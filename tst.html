How a job gets made

Start to finish
01

Compose

Type is set by hand, letter by letter, into a composing stick, then locked into a steel chase.

02

Proof

A single proof pull checks spacing, inking, and impression depth before the full run begins.

03

Run

Sheets feed through the Vandercook one at a time, each pull inked and pressed by hand.

04

Distribute

Once dry, every sort is cleaned and returned to its case — nothing stays set past its job.

Every letter, still in metal.

The case holds three weights of Caslon and a single condensed woodtype face, kept in working order and cleaned after every run. Nothing here is digital — the C, H, and O you see highlighted are the sorts worn softest, the ones pulled most often.

Custom specimen sheets are available on request for clients who want to see a face on paper before committing to a run.
